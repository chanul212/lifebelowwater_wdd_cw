"Restoring the Balance of
Our Blue Ecosystems"

The ocean covers over 70% of our planet, produces half the oxygen we breathe, and supports the livelihoods of over 3 billion people. Yet every day, human activity pushes marine ecosystems closer to collapse. Life Below Water, is our collective commitment to protect, restore, and sustainably use the world's oceans, seas, and marine resources.

The Ocean Crisis

Over 8 million tonnes of plastic enter the ocean every year, and a third of global fish stocks are being harvested unsustainably. Ocean acidification has risen 26% since the Industrial Revolution, destroying coral reefs and marine habitats. The ocean is running out of time.

3 Outcomes If We Act Now:

  • Coral reefs and marine species could stabilise and recover
  • Coastal communities could regain reliable food from healthy fisheries
  • Ocean carbon absorption could be restored, slowing climate change

Who Is Most Affected?

Over 3 billion people rely on the ocean for food and income. In Sri Lanka, more than 2.7 million people depend on fisheries to survive. When fish stocks collapse and coastlines erode, it is everyday families and local communities who suffer most, not large corporations.

3 Actions You Can Take:

  • Choose sustainably sourced, MSC-certified seafood
  • Join a local beach or ocean clean-up
  • Reduce your single-use plastic consumption daily

Solutions That Work

Marine protected areas now cover 8% of the world's oceans, giving ecosystems room to recover. Nations like Palau and Costa Rica have proven that strong ocean policies lead to real ecological recovery. Science, innovation, and community action are already reversing decades of damage.

3 Actions You Can Take:

  • Support marine conservation organisations like Ocean Conservancy
  • Advocate for stronger ocean protection policies locally
  • Spread verified ocean health awareness in your school or community
